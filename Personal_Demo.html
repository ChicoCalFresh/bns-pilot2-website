Personal Demographics

The following set of questions were asked in regards to the students‚Äô personal demographics. This includes the students‚Äô age at the time of the survey (2020), ethnicities they identify as, and the number of dependents they are responsible for taking care of. They were also asked about parents‚Äô education, which will help us identify first generation college students based on certain criteria.

Age

The following was determined by taking the difference between birth date and the time at which the survey was completed (n=407, 98% of 415 reporting). Figure 1.1: Student Age Distribution

The age for participants in the survey range from 20.1 to 60.9 at the time of completion, with the majority of participants being between 20-30 years old. The average age of a participant in this survey is 25.4 years old.

First Generation

Note: students with parents education identified as ‚ÄúOther/Unknown‚Äù were not marked as first gen or not since they could not be determined. (n=379, 91% of 415 reporting).




Figure 1.11: First Generation College Students

From this information, we can see that 359 students who participated in the survey are First Generation College students and 20 students are not First Generation College students.

As defined by the University of California, we define first generation students as ‚Äúworking to be part of the first generation in their families to earn a four-year university degree‚Äù. This means based upon parents education, students in the data set are considered first generation if their parents do not have a Bachelor‚Äôs or Master‚Äôs degree from a four-year university. From the Parents Education graph, all bars highlighted as yellow are considered first gen, while all bars in blue are not (excluding Other/Unknown).
